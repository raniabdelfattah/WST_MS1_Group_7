Japchae Korean Glass Noodles

Japchae is one of those dishes I've been dying to try ever since falling in love with K-Dramas! When I finally tasted it at a buffet restaurant, I was completely hooked. While you can grab japchae from 7/11 stores, I found that the portions are never quite enough and it's so much more economical to make it at home when I want a larger serving. This colorful Korean noodle dish is sort of a stir-fry, sort of a noodle salad - it's tossed together in a bowl rather than cooked on the stove and served barely warm. The chewy, slippery sweet potato noodles combined with tender beef and vibrant vegetables create the perfect comfort food that makes amazing leftovers for lunch too!

Published: October 17, 2025
Recipe by: Helena Tantoco
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 4 people
Difficulty: Medium
Asian Main Dish Side Dish Meat Vegetables

Ingredients

Beef Options (choose one)

  • 600g bone-in beef short ribs (300g boneless) RECOMMENDED
  • 300g boneless beef short ribs RECOMMENDED
  • 300g scotch fillet/boneless rib eye or other steak

Beef Marinade

  • 2 tsp soy sauce (all-purpose or light)
  • 2 tsp white sugar
  • 2 tsp finely minced garlic
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Noodle Dressing

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (all-purpose or light)
  • 2 tsp white sugar
  • 1/2 tsp finely minced garlic
  • 1 1/2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp cooking/kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Noodles & Vegetables

  • 250g sweet potato noodles (dangmyeon), dried
  • 4 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cooking/kosher salt, divided
  • 1 onion, halved and sliced into 6mm wedges
  • 3 green onion stems, cut into 5cm lengths (white & green parts separated)
  • 200g fresh shiitake mushrooms, stems removed, sliced 5mm thick
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into 3mm batons
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 0.5cm slices
  • 4 1/2 cups tightly packed baby spinach
  • 2 tbsp white sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. 1

    Prepare the beef: Cut beef into strips about 5mm thick, 1cm wide, and 5cm long. If using short ribs, trim excess fat and cut meat off the bone, then slice thinly.

  2. 2

    Marinate beef: Place beef in a bowl, add marinade ingredients, and mix well. Set aside for 15-20 minutes.

  3. 3

    Make the sauce: Mix all noodle dressing ingredients in a very large mixing bowl and set aside.

  4. 4

    Cook noodles: Boil sweet potato noodles according to package directions (usually 8 minutes). Drain, briefly rinse under cold water, shake off excess water, and add to the bowl with dressing. DO NOT MIX yet.

  5. 5

    Cook vegetables - Batch 1: Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large non-stick pan over high heat. Cook onion, white parts of green onion, and shiitake mushrooms with 1/2 tsp salt for 2 1/2-3 minutes, stirring constantly until just starting to soften. Pour over noodles but don't mix.

  6. 6

    Cook vegetables - Batch 2: In the same pan over high heat, add 1 tablespoon oil and cook carrots and bell pepper for 1 1/2 minutes, stirring constantly. Add spinach, green onion greens, and remaining 1/2 tsp salt. Cook for 1 1/2 minutes until spinach wilts. Add to noodle bowl but don't mix.

  7. 7

    Cook beef: Heat final 1 tablespoon oil in the same pan over high heat. Add marinated beef and cook for 1 1/2 minutes until lightly golden and just cooked through. Add to noodle bowl.

  8. 8

    Mix and serve: Add most of the sesame seeds (save some for garnish). Now toss everything together thoroughly. Transfer to serving bowl, sprinkle with remaining sesame seeds, and serve warm (not piping hot).

Cooking Tips & Notes

Pro Tip

Cook each component separately and don't mix until the very end to maintain the distinct textures and flavors of each ingredient.

Storage

Leftovers keep for 3 days in the fridge and make excellent lunch portions - not suitable for freezing.

Variations

Substitute sweet potato noodles with glass noodles or vermicelli, use different mushroom varieties, or tenderize cheaper beef cuts with baking soda for 20 minutes before marinating.
